01 / Reset
Under Elliott Hill, Nike is publicly resetting around sport, athletes, and the communities that built the brand — pulling back from over-rotated DTC and returning to wholesale, grassroots, and cultural credibility. Unfazed is that community in physical form: a sold-out room of 2,000+ Gen Z attendees who already live inside Nike's vocabulary — Jordans, Dunks, training fits, basketball.
02 / Audience
Sneakers, basketball, and streetwear are the categories Nike must defend against ON, Hoka, New Balance, and adidas Originals. Unfazed delivers the exact Gen Z sneaker-native cohort that decides what's culturally on or off — and right now, they need to feel that Nike is for them again.
03 / Geography
Atlanta is one of Nike's most important basketball and Jordan Brand markets — and where Gen Z cultural permission is decided. ATL is the cultural capital of US hip-hop and Black streetwear, and Unfazed is the only platform that turns Nike's NA presence into a moment the city talks about for weeks.
04 / Athletes
Nike's edge has always been athlete-led. Atlanta is home to a generation of NBA, WNBA, college, and high-school athletes the Sports Marketing team scouts and signs. Unfazed is the room where those athletes show up off-court, in their fits — the storytelling moment Nike's "win sport back" mandate is supposed to produce.
05 / Pipeline
Nike's grassroots flywheel depends on community access, not media. Unfazed has direct relationships with 1,400 streetwear founders, ATL designers, and youth basketball networks — a private deal-flow for capsule drops, athlete styling moments, and Nike-by-You / customization activations that buy authenticity money can't.
